Splunk Job Posting: Front-end Software Engineer (New Grad/Early Career)

About Splunk

Splunk, a Cisco company, is building a safer and more resilient digital world with an end-to-end full stack platform made for a hybrid, multi-cloud world. Leading enterprises use our unified security and observability platform to keep their digital systems secure and reliable. Our customers love our technology, but it's our caring employees that make Splunk stand out as an amazing career destination. No matter where in the world or what level of the organization, we approach our work with kindness.

Role Summary

Splunk is looking for new grads or early career talent to join our team. As a Front-end Software Engineer, you will work on real projects that go to market in a dynamic, startup-like environment. Gain invaluable experience at a leader in “Big Data”, the hottest software market today.

You will collaborate with cross-functional teams, receive mentorship, and gain insight into our values-driven process—all while supporting your growth and career success.

What You'll Get To Do

  • Design, develop, code, and test software systems or applications for enhancements and new products.
  • Build innovative solutions that enable rapid development.
  • Participate in agile development including project timelines, design specifications, system flow diagrams, documentation, testing, and ongoing support.
  • Recommend modifications to processes and procedures and contribute to standard methodologies, architecture, and implementation.

Qualifications

Must-have:

  • A Bachelor's or Master's in Computer Science, Software Engineering, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Mathematics, or a related technical field with a strong academic record.

Nice-to-have:

  • Strong interest in front-end/UI development
  • Familiarity with JavaScript frameworks like React/Redux (knowledge of Typescript, jQuery, Backbone is a plus)
  • Experience with CSS for styling web applications
  • Familiarity with modern version control systems (e.g., Git)
  • Exposure to test automation tools such as Karma, Mocha, Jest, or Storybook
  • Understanding of CI/CD
  • Experience in agile development environments and effective collaboration
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills with at least 6 months of relevant work or internship experience
